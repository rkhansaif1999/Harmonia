Cookie Policy

What cookies and local storage we use, and why.

Last updated: July 8, 2026

1. What Cookies & Local Storage Are

Cookies are small text files that a website can store on your device to remember information between visits. Similarly, "local storage" is a way for a website to store small amounts of data directly in your browser. This policy covers both, since we use a mix of the two.

2. How We Use Them

Keeping you signed in (local storage)

When you log in, we store your session token and basic account info (like your name and role) in your browser's local storage. This is what lets you stay signed in when you refresh the page or come back later, instead of having to log in every time. This information stays on your device and is sent to our servers only to verify your session — it isn't used for tracking or advertising.

Analytics (cookies)

We use Google Analytics to understand overall traffic and usage patterns on our website — for example, which pages are visited most and how many people land on the site. Google Analytics sets its own cookies in your browser to do this. We use this information in aggregate; we don't use it to build individual advertising profiles.

3. Third-Party Cookies

We use Google Analytics to understand overall site traffic, and Google AdSense to display advertising on the Platform. Google AdSense may set cookies (including on behalf of its advertising partners) to personalize the ads you see and measure their performance. This is governed by Google's own privacy policy and advertising policies. We do not otherwise sell cookie-derived data to third parties.

You can view, adjust, or opt out of personalized advertising at any time through Google's Ad Settings, or via www.aboutads.info. If you are located in the EEA or UK, you may also be shown a cookie consent option that lets you control non-essential cookies, including advertising cookies, before they are set.

4. Managing Cookies

Most browsers let you view, delete, and block cookies through their settings. You can also opt out of Google Analytics specifically using Google's browser add-on, where available. Blocking analytics cookies won't affect your ability to log in or use the Platform, since sign-in relies on local storage rather than tracking cookies. Clearing your browser's local storage will, however, log you out and require you to sign in again.

5. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy as our use of cookies and similar technologies changes. We'll update the "Last updated" date above whenever we do.

6. Contact Us

Questions about this Cookie Policy can be sent to support@harmoniaportal.online or through our Contact page. For more on how we handle personal information generally, see our Privacy Policy.
